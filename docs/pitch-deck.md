## Investor Brief — Micro-Essay Continuous Learning Studio (No-Ghostwriting)

### 1) One-sentence pitch

A subscription product that turns writing into a daily skill loop: users publish short, evidence-backed micro-essays while an LLM acts as a **coach + researcher + sparring partner**—**never a co-author**.

---

### 2) The problem

People want to think and write better, but:

- **Writing is high-friction**: starting, structuring, and revising takes time and confidence.
- **Good feedback is scarce/expensive**: editors, mentors, and knowledgeable peers don’t scale.
- **Research is slow and messy**: people struggle to find credible sources and connect evidence to claims.
- **AI writing tools create a trust gap**: when tools compose/rewite for you, authenticity and integrity are questioned—by readers, employers, and communities. (For example, Grammarly explicitly promotes generative assistance that can compose and rewrite.) ([support.grammarly.com](https://support.grammarly.com/hc/en-us/articles/14528857014285-Introducing-generative-AI-assistance?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

---

### 3) The solution

A micro-essay studio designed for **continuous learning**, not academic assignments.

**Core loop**

1. Pick a prompt (or create one)
2. Write a thesis + a 200–800 word micro-essay
3. Attach evidence (sources → quote snippets → evidence cards)
4. Run “coach review” (rubric + inline comments + questions)
5. Revise and publish
6. Receive anchored objections + iterate

**Non-negotiable principle:** the assistant cannot write any of the essay text. It only:

- flags issues and leaves comments
- asks Socratic questions
- assembles research packets (sources, quotes, summaries)
- extracts argument structure (claims, assumptions, counterclaims)

---

- **Micro formats are culturally native**: people already consume short arguments and explainers; the missing piece is a tool that makes them _better_ and more evidence-driven.

### 6) Product: what’s actually novel

**1) Claim–Evidence linking**
Every claim can be linked to evidence cards (source link + quote snippet + summary + caveats). The system flags mismatches (“citation doesn’t support claim”).

**2) Explainable argument diagnostics**
Instead of “8/10 writing,” we surface actionable issues:

- missing premise
- unsupported claim
- overgeneralization
- weak counterargument handling
- structural confusion

**3) “No-Ghostwriting” enforcement**
The assistant cannot output replacement paragraphs/sentences intended for insertion. It outputs comments, questions, and checklists in a structured schema—an integrity moat vs commoditized writing generators. (Contrast: mainstream tools market composing and rewriting.) ([support.grammarly.com](https://support.grammarly.com/hc/en-us/articles/14528857014285-Introducing-generative-AI-assistance?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

**4) Gamified improvement**
XP is earned for quality behaviors (adding evidence, addressing rebuttals, revising after critique), not raw word count.

**Optional later:** creator monetization rails

- Memberships or paid tracks once retention is proven
- We can initially export/cross-post to existing ecosystems rather than competing head-on with established platforms

---

### 8) Unit economics logic (why it can scale)

LLM spend is controlled by:

- short, structured outputs (comments/checklists, not long prose)
- caching evidence cards
- analyzing diffs on subsequent reviews (only changed sections)
- tiered modes: “Fast Review” vs “Deep Review”
- credit accounting behind the scenes

This is fundamentally cheaper than “generate a whole essay,” because evaluation can be compact and bounded.

### 9) Go-to-market

**Phase 1:** Daily prompt challenge + shareable “I wrote this myself” pages
**Phase 2:** Community distribution (clubs, topic tracks, cohort challenges)
**Phase 3:** Teams (internal micro-memos culture)

A key early growth loop:

1. Publish a micro-essay page with evidence expanders
2. Share it; readers see higher trust signal and clear structure
3. Readers try the daily challenge; streaks drive retention

---

### 10) Risks & mitigations

**Risk: content moderation / platform safety**
Publishing creates moderation load; we start with lightweight publishing and reporting, rate limits, and clear policies.

**Risk: “AI skepticism”**
We lean into it: the tool doesn’t write; it coaches. The integrity stance is a positive differentiator.

**Risk: commoditization**
Moat is in:

- UX (claim–evidence wiring + objections)
- data (which feedback drives real improvement over time)
- community loops (peer objections and revision culture)

---

### 11) Success metrics

- Activation: publish first micro-essay within 24 hours
- Retention: week-4 writer retention; streak continuation
- Quality behaviors: evidence cards per essay; counterargument rate; revision-after-feedback rate
- Monetization: free→pro conversion; churn; ARPU
- Costs: inference cost per weekly active writer; heavy-user rate hitting quotas
- Community health: constructive objection rate; report rate
